# Final Business Recommendation

## Key Findings

The regression analysis identified several factors that are strongly associated with monthly sales.

The strongest predictors are:

- Footfall
- Inventory Availability
- Marketing Spend
- Customer Rating

---

## Leadership Priorities

### Increase Customer Footfall

Footfall is the strongest predictor of sales. Leadership should invest in promotional campaigns, customer engagement initiatives, and store experience improvements to attract more visitors.

### Improve Inventory Availability

Maintaining high inventory availability reduces stockouts and helps maximize sales opportunities.

### Optimize Marketing Spend

Marketing expenditure has a statistically significant positive association with sales. Marketing budgets should be allocated strategically toward stores with the greatest growth potential.

### Enhance Customer Experience

Improving customer satisfaction through better service quality and product availability may contribute to higher monthly sales.

---

## Variables to Interpret Carefully

Regional dummy variables were not statistically significant after controlling for operational factors. Leadership should therefore avoid making major strategic decisions based solely on regional differences.

---

## Risks and Limitations

Regression analysis identifies statistical associations rather than proving causal relationships.

Other important factors such as economic conditions, competitor promotions, weather, seasonal demand, and local events were not included in the model and may also influence monthly sales.

---

## Final Recommendation

The multiple regression model should be used as the primary analytical tool for supporting sales planning and resource allocation.

Business decisions should primarily focus on increasing customer footfall, maintaining inventory availability, improving customer satisfaction, and optimizing marketing investments while considering additional business knowledge beyond the regression results.
