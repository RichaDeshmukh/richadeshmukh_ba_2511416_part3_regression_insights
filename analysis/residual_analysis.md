# Residual Analysis

## Predicted Sales

Predicted monthly sales were calculated using the final multiple regression equation.

## Residual Formula

Residual = Actual Monthly Sales − Predicted Monthly Sales

---

## Largest Positive Residuals

The five stores with the largest positive residuals generated substantially higher sales than predicted by the model.

Possible explanations include:

- Successful local marketing campaigns
- Temporary increases in demand
- Better store management
- Local festivals or events
- Customer loyalty

---

## Largest Negative Residuals

The five stores with the largest negative residuals generated lower sales than predicted.

Possible explanations include:

- Stock shortages
- Staffing issues
- Local competition
- Operational inefficiencies
- External market conditions

---

## Business Interpretation

Most residuals were relatively small compared to total monthly sales, indicating that the final regression model fits the data well.

The presence of a few large residuals suggests that additional variables such as promotional campaigns, economic conditions, or seasonal effects may further improve prediction accuracy.

The model does not consistently over-predict or under-predict any particular region, indicating that operational variables explain sales more effectively than regional differences.
